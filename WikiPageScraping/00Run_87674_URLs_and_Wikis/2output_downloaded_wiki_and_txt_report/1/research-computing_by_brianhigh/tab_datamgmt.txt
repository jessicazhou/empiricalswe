<div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-four-perspectives">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#four-perspectives" id="user-content-four-perspectives"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Four Perspectives</h3>
<div>
<p>We see the term "data management" used in several different ways, generally falling under these four perspectives. We have added parenthetical terms to help distinguish the differences.</p>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-preparatory-data-management">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#preparatory-data-management" id="user-content-preparatory-data-management"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>"(Preparatory) Data Management"</h4>
<div>
<p>Statisticians, data analysts and data scientists tend to use "data management" to mean the specific <em>manipulations</em> needed to <strong>clean-up and restructure</strong> raw data prior to data analysis.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_1" id="user-content-_footnoteref_1" title="View footnote.">1</a>]</sup> They slice, dice, filter, combine, rename, and reshape data as needed for their analysis. Sometimes they refer to this task as data "conditioning", "clean-up", "wrangling", "munging", and "tidying". To a statistician, "managed" data are <em>ready for use</em> in statistical analysis.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_2" id="user-content-_footnoteref_2" title="View footnote.">2</a>]</sup></p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-research-data-management">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#research-data-management" id="user-content-research-data-management"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>"(Research) Data Management"</h4>
<div>
<p>Librarians, archivists, and curators often use "data management" to mean <strong>preserving and sharing</strong> research data in organized, centralized, and searchable systems. They procure, archive, curate, catalog, and publish data.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_3" id="user-content-_footnoteref_3" title="View footnote.">3</a>]</sup> Ask them about <em>metadata</em> and <em>taxonomies</em> to see their eyes light up. They may offer to help you create your <em>data management plan</em> for free. "Managed" data is a data collection which has been preserved and made available as a resource.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_4" id="user-content-_footnoteref_4" title="View footnote.">4</a>]</sup></p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-data-base-management">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#database-management" id="user-content-database-management"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>"Data(base) Management"</h4>
<div>
<p>Computer scientists, information technologists and database administrators (DBAs) think of <strong>designing and managing "databases"</strong>, traditionally "relational database management systems" (RDBMS), and more recently "NoSQL" databases. They model, import, index, and export data, with security, performance, scalability, collaboration, and data integrity in mind. Data which is "managed" is organized and maintained in a properly designed and administered <em>data system</em>, not simply a loose collection of files.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_5" id="user-content-_footnoteref_5" title="View footnote.">5</a>]</sup></p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-enterprise-data-management">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#enterprise-data-management" id="user-content-enterprise-data-management"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>"(Enterprise) Data Management"</h4>
<div>
<p>Professional data managers, corporate IT managers and CIOs will think of "data management" in terms of policies, procedures, and architectures necessary to properly manage the entire data lifecycle of an organization.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_6" id="user-content-_footnoteref_6" title="View footnote.">6</a>]</sup> This includes the building and operation of <strong>enterprise data systems</strong>, consisting of operational systems specifically designed and optimized for routine transactions and one large, centralized, integrated system or "data warehouse" optimized for long-term storage and analysis.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_7" id="user-content-_footnoteref_7" title="View footnote.">7</a>]</sup> To them, data worth collecting is worth "managing" to ensure consistency, security, and usability.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>How do <strong><em>you</em></strong> think of data management? As a scientific researcher, we hope you consider all of these perspectives as you conduct your research. Plan to spend time <em>organizing</em> your data according to your <em>requirements</em>, allocate computing and other <em>resources</em> to the task, <em>budget</em> accordingly, and think about your <em>collaborators</em> and how you might make <em>accessing</em> the data easier (and more secure) for the entire team. Most importantly, consider data management <em>early</em> in your project. Design your experiment with data management in mind.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_8" id="user-content-_footnoteref_8" title="View footnote.">8</a>]</sup> What data will be most helpful in answering your research question? How will you collect, store, process, and publish your data most effectively?</p>
</div>
